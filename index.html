How to Execute Each Strategy Type
Day Trade (0DTE) Execution Guide
When to enter: First 30 min after open, or during major news events
Position sizing: Risk no more than 1-2% of account per trade
Stop loss: Set at 50% of premium paid (hard stop)
Profit target: 50-100% gain on premium, scale out in thirds
Key rules:
- Only trade 0DTE on high-liquidity underlyings (SPX, SPY, QQQ, TSLA)
- Never hold 0DTE overnight
- Expect 100% loss of premium is possible on every trade
- Use defined-risk spreads when possible
- Watch for theta crush acceleration after 2 PM ET
Swing Trade Execution Guide
Time horizon: 5-20 trading days
DTE selection: Choose 2-3x your expected hold time (hold 2 weeks = buy 30-45 DTE)
Position sizing: 2-5% of account per position
Entry: Use technical levels (support/resistance, moving averages)
Management:
- Take 50% off at 50% profit
- Move stop to breakeven after first target hit
- Close if underlying reverses key technical level
- Close at 21 DTE if not profitable (theta accelerates)
- Use spreads to reduce cost basis and define risk
LEAP Execution Guide
Time horizon: 6-24 months
DTE selection: 9-24 month expiration
Strike selection: Deep ITM (70-85 delta) for stock replacement, or ATM for aggressive growth bet
Position sizing: 5-15% of account (per position, max 3-4 LEAP positions)
Management:
- Roll forward when < 90 DTE remaining
- Sell short-dated calls against LEAPs (PMCC) for income
- Dollar-cost average if underlying drops 10%+
- Take profit at 100%+ gain or re-evaluate thesis
- Buy on high-volume pullbacks in quality names
- PMCC: Sell 30-45 DTE calls at 0.20-0.30 delta against your LEAP
Premium Selling Guide (High IV Environment)
Current VIX: 28 (Elevated - SELLER'S MARKET)
Best strategies: Iron Condors, Credit Spreads, Short Strangles
DTE selection: 30-45 days to expiration
Wing width: Sell at 1 standard deviation (16 delta)
Position sizing: Max risk = 2-3% of account per trade
Management:
- Close at 50% of max profit (don't get greedy)
- Close at 21 DTE if not at 50% profit
- Adjust if short strike is tested (roll untested side in)
- Never let a position go to max loss - manage at 2x credit received
- High IV = widen your wings for more premium and higher probability
Risk Management Rules CRITICAL
NEVER risk more than you can afford to lose.
Options can expire worthless. 100% loss of premium is a real possibility on every single trade.
Position Sizing:
  • Day trades: Max 1-2% of account per trade
  • Swings: Max 2-5% per position
  • LEAPs: Max 5-15% per position
  • Total portfolio risk: Never exceed 20-30% in options
Stop Loss Rules:
  • 0DTE: Stop at 50% of premium
  • Swings: Stop at 30-50% loss on premium
  • Credit spreads: Close at 2x credit received in loss
  • LEAPs: Re-evaluate thesis if underlying drops 15%+
Current Market Warnings:
  • VIX at 28 = options are EXPENSIVE to buy
  • Geopolitical risk can cause overnight gaps
  • Oil at $93+ creates broad market headwinds
  • FOMC next week - expect volatility spike
  • High VIX = great for SELLING premium

IMC Trading Earnings IV Crush Strategy VOLATILITY

How Earnings IV Crush Works

Before earnings, IV inflates as uncertainty rises. After the announcement, uncertainty resolves and IV collapses 30-60% overnight, regardless of stock direction. Selling options 1-3 days before earnings and buying them back the morning after captures this IV crush as pure profit.

Earnings Playbook

1. Entry: 1-3 days before earnings (peak IV)
2. Strategy: Iron condor (neutral) or strangle (neutral)
3. Strikes: Set outside expected move
4. Size: Max 1-2% risk (earnings are binary)
5. Exit: Close at market open the morning after earnings
6. Target: 20-40% of credit collected from IV crush alone

Upcoming Earnings Calendar

Week of Apr 13: Major bank earnings (JPM, GS, MS)
Week of Apr 20: Tech earnings begin (NFLX, TSLA)
Week of Apr 27: Big tech (AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, META, AMZN)
Start watching IV expansion 7-10 days before report dates. Enter iron condors 1-3 days prior.
